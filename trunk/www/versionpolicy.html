OpenMark version number policy

We use a linux-like policy.

  • Stable releases are have numbers like 1.2.0, 1.4.0, 1.6.0, with an even second number.
  • Minor bug and security fixes to the 1.4.0 release will be numbered 1.4.1, 1.4.2, etc. and developed off a stable branch in subversion.
  • After the 1.4.0 release, new development will take place on a 1.5.x, series of development snap-shots, which will lead up to the 1.6.0 release.

On the status page of a running OpenMark server, the version number displayed will look like

Om version: 1.4.0 (2007-05-09 18:15:11)

Where the timestamp in brackets was the time the system was compiled.

The version number of the codebase is stored in version.properties file.

At the moment, all the development is being done for the Open Univeristy. Therefore, there will be a new stable version every time the OU's live servers are updated, and a sequence of developer snapshots during development and testing of that release. If other people join the development effort, this will, of course, change.
